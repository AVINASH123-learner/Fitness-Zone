Protein

  • Proteins are responsible for many processes such as growing, healing,
    forming cell structure, growing hair and nails, providing energy and more.
  • Protein helps make a meal more satiating which, in turn,
    helps people maintain a healthy weight.
  • Protein exists in every one of the trillions of cells in the human body.
    Without it, no life could exist. Approximately 18-20% of the body is protein.
  • Protein, like fat and carbohydrates, is a macronutrient.
    All foods are made up of these macronutrients and therefore energy.











CARBOHYDRATE

  • The brain is the only carbohydrate-dependent organ in the body.
    The brain exclusively uses glucose, a basic simple sugar carbohydrate, to function.
  • Carbohydrates are the only fuel source metabolized fast enough to support hard exercise.
  • The chemical structure of carbohydrates is a carbon backbone with hydrogen and
    oxygen molecules attached.
  • Carbohydrates are the most important energy source for the body.
    It stores them in reserves to make you use them, even if you stop feeding them to yourself.











Dietary Fibre

  • High-fiber foods tend to be more filling than low-fiber foods,
    so you're likely to eat less and stay satisfied longer.
  • In people with diabetes, fiber can slow the absorption of sugar and
    help improve blood sugar levels.
  • Dietary fiber increases the weight and size of your stool and softens it
    decreasing your chance of constipation.
  • Dietary fiber is probably best known for its ability to prevent or relieve constipation.







Minerals

  • Iron is an essential mineral, primarily found in the blood,
    and stored in your liver, spleen, bone marrow and muscles.
  • Zinc is an essential mineral that is important for keeping your immune system
    strong and helps your body fight infections, heal wounds and repair cells.
  • The minerals phosphorus and magnesium, calcium gives your bones strength
    and density. This mineral also builds and maintains strong, healthy teeth
  • Minerals are used for making enzymes and hormones.
    There are two kinds of minerals: macrominerals and trace minerals.







Vitamin

  • Water-soluble vitamins are not stored in the body.
    The nine water-soluble vitamins are vitamin C and all the B vitamins.
  • Fat-soluble vitamins are stored in the body's liver, fatty tissue, and muscles.
    The four fat-soluble vitamins are vitamins A, D, E, and K.
  • Vitamins can be found in both plant-based and animals-based food products.
    Vitamin deficiency may cause some diseases and its overdose also causes diseases
  • Vitamins are organic compounds that are needed for normal cell function,
    growth, and development. Vitamins can be fat-soluble or water-soluble.







Water

  • Since your brain is mostly water, drinking it helps you think,
    focus and concentrate better and be more alert
  • Water can help provide a feeling of fullness because water adds volume
  • Water plays many important roles in the body such as
    transportation of nutrients and is necessary for digestion.
  • Water is one of the 6 essential nutrients.
    Around 60 percent of our body is made up of water.
